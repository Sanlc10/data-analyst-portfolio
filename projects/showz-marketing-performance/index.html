How we found the channels worth spending on.

An end-to-end audit of the user funnel and marketing spend for Showz, an online events ticketing platform.

Dataset3 tables · 2017–18 Tools DomainMarketing Analytics Year2025
Business context

A ticketing platform with a marketing problem.

Showz operates an online events ticketing marketplace. Like most early-growth platforms, they had a clear top of funnel but blurry attribution downstream: marketing was spending across multiple acquisition channels, but no one could confidently answer which channels were generating profitable customers — and which were just buying traffic.

The brief was deliberately open: build the lens through which Showz can evaluate marketing performance, end to end. That meant going from raw event logs all the way to a defensible recommendation on where the next marketing dollar should go.

Sources 1 and 2 generate 70% of revenue on under 30% of the spend. Source 3 is doing the opposite.

Methodology

A funnel-to-finance pipeline.

01

Data cleaning

Standardised three datasets — visits, orders and ad costs. Normalised datetime fields and added month/week derived features.

02

Engagement metrics

Built DAU, WAU and MAU. Layered session length and conversion windows on top.

03

Cohort analysis

Cohorted users by acquisition month. Tracked time-to-first-purchase and retention curves per cohort.

04

Unit economics

Computed LTV, CAC and ROMI per acquisition source. Cross-referenced with channel cost data.

05

Recommendation

Ranked channels by LTV : CAC ratio and translated findings into a budget reallocation proposal.

Key insights

Three numbers that change the budget.

Platform engagement baseline

Average of 23,228 monthly active users. Daily active users grew from 845 in 2017 to 997 in 2018.

110×ROMI
Source 1 — best return on spend

Source 1 returns $110 for every $1 of marketing spend — LTV $322 against CAC $2.92. Source 2 is the close second (62×).

2.1×ROMI
Source 3 — where the budget went

Source 3 received the largest investment but returned only $2.10 per dollar. CAC ($10.21) exceeds LTV ($21.43).

Daily active users — 2017 vs 2018
Average daily active users grew from 845 (2017) to 997 (2018) — an 18% increase. Weekly and monthly averages: 5,716 WAU and 23,228 MAU.

Total revenue attributed to each source_id over the analysis window. Source 7 (one customer, zero ad spend) is excluded.
LTV vs CAC by acquisition source

Final takeaway
Recommendation

If I were sitting on Showz's marketing team, I'd pull spend off Source 3 and shift it toward Sources 1, 2 and 5 — the three channels where LTV outruns CAC by 20× or more. Source 3's problem isn't volume; it's audience quality, and no extra budget will rescue a $10.21 CAC on a $21.43 LTV.
